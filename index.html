Personal Data in Art Galleries

The PhD project explores how art galleries could lead the way for a new, ethical, transparent, and reciprocal way of collecting data.
To do this, I'm asking the gallery-visiting public to talk to me about what personal data they would be happy to give to galleries, and what they would want in return!

Personal Data

Personal data is collected about us every single day, from dozens of different sources. It can vary from your name and address, to your fingerprints, to how you get to work in the mornings! This data can be used for good, or for bad, depending on who is collecting and why.

Power

That personal data has power, but art galleries have power too, and so do you! Sometimes, data is used in ways that are hidden or deceptive, preventing the power from being shared equally. By shedding light on these practices, we will be able to redistribute power to make sure you have control over what happens to your data.

Why Art Galleries?

Art galleries are uniquely situated at the forefront of culture to share information to a huge number of people. They are able to disseminate and spread information about your rights as data subjects, and to encourage healthier and more beneficial practices surrounding the use of your personal data.

How can you help?

I want to hear your thoughts about how we can make data collection better

Working together, we can develop a new way of collecting data which is clear, helps you learn about data collection and your rights
as data subjects, and gives you something in return for your data. This next section will explain in a little more depth what you can do to help.

How to get involved in the project

If you have visited an art gallery in the last 18 months, and are 18 years old or more, then I want to hear from you! You don't need any existing knowledge about personal data or anything of the sort; I'll make sure you know everything that you need to know throughout the course of the online activities.

Taking part will include watching a total of six short videos where I talk a little bit about what personal data is, how it relates to power, what the project hopes to achieve, and also where I set you some activities to complete online - more on that in a second. Before taking part I will ask you to complete some paperwork, including reading a short information sheet, and filling in a consent form to say you're happy to take part.

So the first two short videos will set the scene, and then the remaining four will

each set you one activity to complete as soon as you are able to. If you decide totake part, please do complete all of the actitivies, although you are welcome to fit them in around your other commitments and take your time in doing so. In total, participation should take around 40-45 minutes.

The first activity will ask you to fill in an individual worksheet that only you and I will see using a website called mural.co - their privacy policy can be found here. Then, the next three activities will ask you to complete collaborative documents using the same website - these won't involve any personal data, just questions like what kinds of data would you be willing to donate to galleries? These documents will be filled in by all the participants, so you'll be able to see what other people are thinking and decide if you agree or not!

Before you make any decision about being involved, there is some paperwork I recommend reading. Click here to see the official information sheet. This info sheet

includes more information about how your data will be used and your rights to withdraw from the study should you wish.

Click here to see the University of Nottingham's privacy notice which explains in depth how your data is processed, stored, shared, and protected within the University, as well as laying out your rights under GDPR.

Finally, before we begin, you will need to complete and return to me a consent form which you will receive via email once you've been in touch. If you have any questions about the form, do just send me an email and I'll be more than happy to talk you through it and what it all means (preferably before ticking no!)

The Activities

By now, you should have read the information sheet and privacy notice, and signed and returned to me a consent form. So it's time to get into the fun stuff! To the right of this text, you'll see six images. Each of these images will link you to a YouTube video which you will need to watch. So when you watch Video Three, you also need to have open Activity One - Silhouettes; Video four links to Activity Two - Identifying Data; Video Five to Activity Three - Value of Data; and Video Six to Activity Four - Reciprocation.

Thank you again for agreeing to take part in this study - it really means a lot to me, and hopefully something truly good will come from your time and dedication today. As always, I am available through email if you have any question, comments, or follow up. Thank you! - Harriet

About me and contact info

I am an interdisciplinary PhD researcher at the Mixed Reality Lab, University of Nottingham, based in the Horizon CDT.
I come from a background in Human Geography, where I studied digital identity, virtual communities, and online activism.
This fundamentally shaped my approach to research, which continues to explore identity, space, place, and how
technologies interact with and configure these experiential concepts. Currently, my research centres around using
technologies to enable more meaningful relationships to flourish between art, audiences, and cultural venues.
